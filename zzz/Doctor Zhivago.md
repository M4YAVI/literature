
## 1. Childhood and early life (late 1800s)

Yuri Zhivago is orphaned young. His mother dies, his father disappears after financial scandals. Yuri is taken in by wealthy family friends, the Gromekos, in Moscow. He grows up sensitive, intelligent, and artistic. He studies medicine but is also drawn to poetry and philosophy.

Russia is unstable. Social unrest is growing. Yuri feels it but doesn’t fully engage yet.

---

## 2. Lara’s story begins (parallel timeline)

Larissa (“Lara”) Antipova grows up poor. Her mother runs a dress shop. A powerful lawyer and political fixer, Victor Komarovsky, becomes her mother’s lover and gradually manipulates Lara. He exploits her emotionally and sexually.

Lara becomes conflicted, ashamed, and angry. She tries to escape his control but is pulled back repeatedly.

---

## 3. Revolution of 1905

Political unrest erupts. Protests, strikes, violence.

Lara becomes involved with revolutionary circles. She meets Pavel (“Pasha”) Antipov, an idealistic young revolutionary. They fall in love.

Meanwhile, Yuri witnesses violence firsthand while traveling. His romantic view of life begins to fracture.

---

## 4. Lara, Komarovsky, and the breaking point

Komarovsky continues manipulating Lara. She attempts to free herself. At a Christmas party, Lara tries to shoot Komarovsky. She misses. The incident becomes infamous but is quietly covered up due to Komarovsky’s influence.

Lara leaves Moscow with Pasha. They marry and try to start over.

---

## 5. Yuri becomes a doctor and marries

Yuri completes medical school. He marries Tonia Gromeko, his childhood companion. Their marriage is affectionate, stable, and conventional. They have a son.

Yuri works as a physician and writes poetry privately.

---

## 6. World War I

Russia enters World War I. Yuri is drafted as an army doctor. Lara also serves at the front as a nurse, searching for Pasha, who has gone missing in combat.

Yuri and Lara meet at the front. They work together in brutal conditions. They form a deep emotional connection, restrained but intense. Both are married. Nothing happens physically, but the bond is clear.

Eventually, Yuri returns home.

---

## 7. The October Revolution and its aftermath

The Bolshevik Revolution overturns Russian society. Private property is seized. Moscow becomes dangerous and impoverished.

Yuri’s family loses their home. Food is scarce. Life becomes bleak.

Pasha, presumed dead, has actually survived and reinvented himself as Strelnikov, a ruthless Bolshevik military commander. He is cold, driven, and ideologically rigid.

---

## 8. Flight to the countryside

Yuri, Tonia, their children, and relatives flee Moscow for the Ural region, hoping for stability. They settle at a former family estate in Varykino.

Yuri tries to live quietly, practicing medicine and writing.

Lara is nearby, now living alone with her daughter. She and Yuri reconnect.

---

## 9. Yuri and Lara’s affair

Yuri and Lara begin an affair. It is emotionally intense and short-lived. Both know it cannot last.

Their time together is overshadowed by war, political terror, and moral conflict.

---

## 10. Yuri captured by partisans

Yuri is forcibly conscripted by a Red partisan group. He spends over a year with them, serving as a doctor while witnessing brutality, executions, and ideological fanaticism.

He becomes disillusioned with revolutionary ideals. He survives by keeping his head down.

---

## 11. After captivity

Yuri escapes and returns to Lara. They live together briefly. Their relationship is now open and resigned rather than hopeful.

Komarovsky reappears, now aligned with the new regime. He offers Lara and her daughter safe passage out of Russia. Yuri refuses to go, believing it wrong and impossible for him.

Lara leaves with Komarovsky, believing Yuri will follow. He never does.

This is their final separation.

---

## 12. Yuri’s decline

Yuri returns to Moscow alone. Tonia and their children have already been deported abroad. He receives a farewell letter from her.

Yuri lives in poverty. His health deteriorates. He drifts between jobs and relationships, unable to recover his former life or purpose.

---

## 13. Yuri’s death

Yuri dies suddenly of a heart attack on a Moscow street, unnoticed by most passersby.

A small group attends his funeral. Lara appears briefly, devastated. She disappears afterward, likely arrested or killed in the purges. Her fate is unknown.

---

## 14. Epilogue (years later)

After World War II, Yuri’s half-brother meets a young woman who may be the daughter of Yuri and Lara. Her identity is uncertain.

The novel ends with Yuri’s poems, emphasizing individual conscience, creativity, and moral freedom over ideology.

---

## What the novel is saying (briefly)

- History crushes private lives.
- Ideology dehumanizes people.
- Personal conscience matters more than political loyalty.
- Love and art survive, but barely.

That’s _Doctor Zhivago_.  
Long book. Sad people. Bad timing. History wins.
